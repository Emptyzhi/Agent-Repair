A First-Time Visitor's Guide to the Italian Bidet

A typical Italian hotel bidet, featuring separate hot and cold water controls.

1. What is a Bidet and Why is it Used?

A bidet is a plumbing fixture, separate from a toilet, designed for personal hygiene. Its primary purpose is to wash the perineal and anal areas after using the toilet. The fixture uses a stream of water to provide a more thorough and hygienic cleaning than toilet paper alone. It is a standard fixture in most Italian and many other European homes and hotels, where it is considered an essential part of daily personal cleanliness.

2. How to Use the Bidet: A Step-by-Step Guide

Using a bidet for the first time can be confusing, but the process is straightforward. The model shown features separate taps for hot and cold water, which is common in Italy.

  1. Use the Toilet First: The bidet is for cleaning, not for waste. Use the toilet as you normally would first. Some people prefer a preliminary wipe with toilet paper before moving to the bidet.
  2. Position Yourself: Straddle the bidet, usually facing the controls and faucet. This position offers the best control over the water flow and temperature. You may need to remove your trousers and underwear to do this comfortably.
  3. Adjust the Water: Slowly turn on the hot and cold taps to achieve a comfortable water temperature. Start with the cold water first to avoid scalding, then gradually add hot water. Adjust the flow to a gentle, comfortable pressure.
  4. Cleanse: Lean forward and use your hand to direct the water and cleanse the desired areas. Soap is often used, typically a mild, pH-neutral intimate wash, which is often provided in hotels.
  5. Dry Off: After washing, dry the area thoroughly. Hotels provide a specific small towel, often called a "bidet towel," for this purpose. It is for personal use and is changed daily. Alternatively, you can use toilet paper.

3. What Not to Do with a Bidet

To ensure proper use and avoid damage or plumbing issues, please adhere to the following:

  • Do Not Use it as a Toilet: The bidet is not designed to flush solid waste or toilet paper.
  • Do Not Use Harsh Chemicals: Avoid using harsh soaps, shampoos, or other chemicals that are not intended for intimate hygiene.
  • Do Not Throw Foreign Objects in the Bowl: The drain is not equipped to handle anything other than water and a small amount of soap. Do not dispose of wipes, sanitary products, or other items in the bidet.
  • Do Not Leave the Water Running: Turn the taps off completely after use.

4. Cultural Norms in Italy and Europe

In Italy, using a bidet is a deeply ingrained part of the daily hygiene routine for all ages and is considered essential for feeling clean and fresh. It is not seen as a luxury but as a standard of personal care. This sentiment is shared in many other European countries like Spain, Portugal, and Greece. When visiting an Italian home, you are expected to know what a bidet is and how to use it. Not using it might be considered unhygienic by your hosts. The presence of a bidet towel is standard, and it is meant for drying after bidet use only.
